H5N1 in Poultry in North Korea
 The North Korean Director of the Veterinary and, Ministry of Agriculture, Anti-Epizootic Department, confirmed an outbreak of H5N1 at a duck factory in Pyongyang.  After a cage of ducks showed signs of being infected back in April, and mortality continuing throughout the month, samples were sent to the lab and came back positive last week.  All 16, 400 susceptible ducks, including more than 2,000 adult ducks, 42,000 fattening ducks and 120,000 ducklings younger than 20 days, were dead or killed.  Control measures being applied include stamping out, screening, movement control throughout the country, and disinfection of contaminated areas.
