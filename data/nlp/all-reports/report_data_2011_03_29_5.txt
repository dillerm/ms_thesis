H5N1 in Poultry in Japan
 OIE recently reported 84 new cases of H5N1 bird flu in poultry in Japan: 82 cases from a poultry farm in Wakaba-ku, and 2 cases from another poultry farm in the same region.  All 84 birds died from the infection.  Poultry from the first farm tested positive for the virus on March 12 by rapid testing, and poultry from the second farm tested positive for infection on March 16 by PCR testing. Mandatory movement restriction has been enforced within 10 km of the affected farms since the days of the respective confirmed infections.  The source of the outbreaks is unknown, but birds are being screened for the disease.
