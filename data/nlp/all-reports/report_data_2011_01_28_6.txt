H5N1 in Poultry in South Korea
The Ministry for Food, Agriculture, Forestry and Fisheries confirmed two new bird flu outbreaks late on January 24, one involving an egg-laying chicken farm in Sangju, North Gyeongsang, which had not been affected by bird flu in the past. All 201,000 chickens at the Sangju farm will be culled along with 8,300 ducks at a farm in Icheon, Gyeonggi.The Ministry for Food, Agriculture, Forestry and Fisheries reported that two new outbreaks of bird flu have been confirmed at a chicken farm and a pheasant farm in Pyeongtaek, about 70 km south of Seoul. All 25,000 chickens and 2,000 pheasants on the properties have been culled as a precautionary measure. Counting the new cases, the country has reported 40 outbreaks since the first was confirmed on December 31 and more than 5.15 million birds have been culled so far.
