China (LPAI H7N9)
The United Nations World Health Organization (WHO) said Thursday that China's National Health and Family Planning Commission has notified it of two new laboratory-confirmed case of human infection with avian influenza A(H7N9) virus in the country, including one death. The deceased patient was a 66-year-old woman from Xinjiang Uyghur Autonomous Region. She had onset of symptoms on July 14, was admitted to a hospital on July 17. She died on August 3. The patient had a history of exposure to live poultry. The other patient is a 53-year-old male, also from Xinjiang Uyghur Autonomous Region. He had onset of symptoms on August 5 and was admitted to a hospital on August 9. His symptoms continue to be mild. He has a history of exposure to live poultry. The Chinese government is continuing to take surveillance and control measures, reinforcing case management and treatment as well as conducting risk communication with the public.
