H5N1 in Poultry in Bangladesh
Bird flu broke out on a commercial poultry farm in Dhaka division on January 1, killing 530 birds on a farm of 6500. The remaining birds were culled to prevent the spread of the disease. The origin of the outbreak is unknown, but preventative measures such as stamping out, movement control within the country, and disinfection of the premises have been put in place. Tests by the Bangladesh Livestock Research Institute and the Central Disease Investigation Laboratory in Dhaka were positive for H5N1 bird flu.
