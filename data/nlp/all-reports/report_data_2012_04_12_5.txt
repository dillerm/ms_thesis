H5N1 in Humans in Egypt
The Ministry of Health and Population of Egypt has notified WHO of two new cases of human infection with H5N1 bird flu virus. The first patient is a 2-year-old girl from Demiatta Governorate. She developed symptoms on March 19, was admitted to a hospital on March 20, and received oseltamivir. She is still being treated and is in good medical condition. The case was confirmed by the Central Public Health Laboratories on March 22. Epidemiological investigations into the source of infection indicate that the girl had been exposed to dead backyard poultry. The second patient, a 15 year-old girl from Giza Governorate, developed symptoms on March 25, was admitted to a hospital in critical condition on March 29, received oseltamivir on admission, and died on March 31. The case was confirmed by the Central Public Health Laboratories on March 31. Epidemiological investigations into the source of infection are ongoing.
