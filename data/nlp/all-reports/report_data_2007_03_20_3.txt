H5N1 in Domestic Animals in Afghanistan:
Over the past week, 13 new cases of bird flu have been detected in Afghanistan, bringing the number of confirmed cases in the country to 17 for 2007, health officials said.  Assadullah Azhari, a spokesman for the United Nations Food and Agriculture Organisation in Kabul, reported that 9 dead birds were diagnosed with the virulent H5N1 strain in 5 districts of Nangarhar and Kunar provinces.  Four other cases were identified in backyard poultry in the capital of Kabul.  In an effort to reduce the risks of the virus, authorities have launched a massive poultry vaccination campaign in the capital and in the 2 provinces where the infectious disease has been confirmed.
