H5N1 in Wild Birds in Vietnam
 An outbreak of highly pathogenic avian influenza (HPAI) H5N1 has been reported in the city of Phan Rang-Tháp Chàm, in the Ninh Thuan province of Vietnam.  Officials are reporting that between March 28 and April 11, nearly 5,000 swifts have died after contacting the H5N1 virus.  Swift nests, made mostly of the birds’ saliva, are a prized ingredient for soup and other delicacies throughout the area.  Officials are currently monitoring the situation. If confirmed this would be the first time that any swifts is infected with HPAI H5N1.
