H5N1 in Wild birds in Hong Kong
A little egret (Egretta garzetta) found on November 18 at Tuen Mun Park in the New Territories has tested positive for H5N1, the Agriculture, Fisheries and Conservation Department reported; the egret died the day after it was found. This is the first time H5N1 has been detected in Hong Kong since the beginning of the year when 17 other wild birds were found with the virus.
