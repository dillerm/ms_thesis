H5N1 in Poultry in Myanmar
A farm in Sagaing region is experiencing an outbreak of H5N1 bird flu that was confirmed by the Regional Veterinary Diagnostic Laboratory in Mandalay on February 24 and by the Central Veterinary Diagnostic Laboratory in Yangon on February 26. The farm contained a total of 1,060 layer chickens in two establishments. A few chickens were found dead on February 20 and a total of 61 chickens became sick and died within four days. The remaining 999 birds were culled as a precautionary measure. Officials have applied other control measures including quarantine, movement control, zoning, disinfection of the premises, and dipping/spraying. The cause of the outbreak is currently unknown.
