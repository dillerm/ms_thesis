H5N1 in Poultry in Tibet
 The National Avian Influenza Reference Laboratory on Monday confirmed an outbreak of H5N1 on a farm in Linzhi, located in southwestern China's Tibet Autonomous Region.  Of the 407 birds, including chicken and geese, 35 died while the remaining 372 were culled to prevent the disease from spreading.  Control measures being applied include stamping out, movement control around the country, screening and zoning, and disinfection of infected areas.
