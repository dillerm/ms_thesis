H5N1 in Humans in UK
An ecclesiastical court in Britain has authorized scientists to exhume the body of a man who died in the 1918 flu epidemic.  The body of Sir Mark Sykes, a British aristocrat and diplomat, was exhumed on the 15th as part of medical research to test genetic material from the 1918 virus.  Sykes? body was buried in a lead-lined coffin which may have preserved the tissues needed for testing. This is the first time the ecclesiastical court has approved an exhumation for medical research.(more...)
