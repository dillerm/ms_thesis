H5N1 in Poultry in Nepal
In the Bhaktapur District of Nepal, an outbreak of H5N1 bird flu at a poultry farm was confirmed on October 13th. After chickens began dying early in the week of October 7th, samples were sent to the Animal Health Laboratory in Nepal, verifying the birds were infected with highly pathogenic avian influenza. According to the report submitted to OIE, the outbreak may have began as early as Aug 27. The commercial later farm had dual purpose breeds of different age groups. Out of almost 2,000 chickens, 1,200 died from the disease, while on October 15, the remaining 780 were culled. The Directorate of Animal Health began disinfecting the area on Tuesday and will be increasing surveillance and monitoring of poultry farms near the infected locale. The District Public Health Office is conducting health screenings of local residents as well as educating the public on methods to avoid infection.
