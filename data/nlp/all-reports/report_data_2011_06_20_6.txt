H5N1 in Humans in Egypt
The Ministry of Health in Egypt notified the World Health Organization of five recent human cases of infection with H5N1 bird flu. The first patient is a 40-year-old woman from Aswan District, Aswan Governorate. She developed symptoms on May 14, was hospitalized and treated with oseltamivir. She recovered and was discharged. The second patient was a 21-year-old pregnant woman from Ashmun District, Minufiyah Governorate. She developed symptoms on May 21, was hospitalized and received oseltamivir, but died on May 29. The third patient was a 31-year-old man from Shobra el Kheima District, Qalyubiyah Governorate. He developed symptoms on May 21, was hospitalized, and received oseltamivir, but died on June 5. The fourth patient was a 32-year-old man from el Zawya District, Cairo Governorate. He developed symptoms on May 23, was hospitalized, and received oseltamivir, but died on June 2. The fifth case is a 16-year-old boy from Ashmun District, Minufiyah Governorate. He developed symptoms on May 21 and was hospitalized. He received oseltamivir and is in critical condition, but he is recovering. Investigations into the source of infection indicate that all the patients were exposed to poultry suspected to have avian influenza. The cases were confirmed by the Egyptian Central Public Health Laboratories, a National Influenza Center of the WHO Global Influenza Surveillance Network. Of the 149 cases confirmed to date in Egypt, 51 have been fatal.
