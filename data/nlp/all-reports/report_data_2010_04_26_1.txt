H1N1 in South Korea
On April 12 H5N1 flu broke out on two farms in Kyonggi-Do province. Altogether the farms housed 3,800 swine and 36 animals were infected. The infections were discovered during routine monitoring; the origin of the disease is unknown. Tests were conducted by the National Veterinary Research and Quarantine Service. Measures such as movement control inside the country and disinfection of the premises have been taken to prevent the spread of the disease.
