H5N1 in Wild birds in Hong Kong
A peregrine falcon (Falco peregrinus calidus) found dead on March 12 and a house crow (Corvus splendens) found dead on March 15 both tested positive for H5N1 bird flu. An intensive surveillance system is in place for all poultry farms, poultry markets and pet bird shops in Hong Kong. The H5N1 infected wild birds were detected as part of an ongoing surveillance program.Tests were conducted by Tai Lung Veterinary Laboratory and the Centre of Influenza Research in Hong Kong.
