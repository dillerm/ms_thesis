H5N1 in Wild birds in Japan
OIE recently reported 17 new cases of H5N1 bird flu in wild birds in Japan; 1 in Nagasaki, 2 in Hokkaido, 1 in Hyogo, 1 in Kyoto, 1 in Tokushima, 3 in Oita, 1 in Kagoshima, 4 in Miyazaki, 1 in Fukushima, 1 in Tochigi, and 1 in Yamaguchi district. A total of 19 dead wild birds tested positive for bird flu between February 7 and February 21; 1 black swan (Cygnus atratus), 5 peregrine falcons (Falco peregrinus), 1 tundra swan (Cygnus columbianus), 5 mandarin ducks (Aix galericula), 1 hooded crane (Grus monacha), 1 little grebe (Tacybaptus ruficollis), 1 grey heron (Ardea cinerea), 1 Ural owl (Strix uralensis), 1 mute swan (Cygnus olor), 1 whooper swan (Cygnus cygnus), and 1 greater scaup (Aythya marila). The source of the outbreak is unknown.
