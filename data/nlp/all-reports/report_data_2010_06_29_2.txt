H5N1 in Poultry in Bangladesh
An outbreak of H5N1 bird flu began June 17 in Rajshahi division, killing 332 birds on a farm of 5,621. The rest were culled as a preventative measure. Samples tested positive for H5N1 at the Bangladesh Livestock Research Institute and the Field Disease Investigation Laboratory in Joypurhat. The source of the infection is unknown, but measures such as movement control inside the country and disinfection of the premises have been undertaken to prevent the spread of the disease.
