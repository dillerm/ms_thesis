LPAI H7N9 in Wild birds in China
 Fear of another outbreak of the H7N9 bird flu virus was heightened after two wild geese were found dead at the Shanghai Zoo earlier this week; however, officials confirmed that the two birds actually died of intestinal inflammation and hepatitis.  To date, 33 H7N9 cases and 15 deaths have been confirmed in Shanghai to bring the total to 132 cases with 38 deaths.  The most recent victim, a 59-year-old man, died on May 31st after being diagnosed at the beginning of April.
