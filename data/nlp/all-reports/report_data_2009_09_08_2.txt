H5N1 in Poultry in Indonesia
400 chickens infected with avian flu in the village of Tejakula on Bali were culled to prevent the spread of the disease. Bali Husbandry Agency head I.B. Alit said, "Intensive culling must be carried out in two affected villages in the regency." He added that avian flu is still present in domestic fowl on the island. A pregnant woman from Gianyar regency is being treated for bird flu at Sanglah Hospital. The fetus was killed by the virus.
