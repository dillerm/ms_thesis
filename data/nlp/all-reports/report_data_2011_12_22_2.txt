H5N1 in Poultry in Bangladesh
 In the Khulna and Dhaka Divisions of Bangladesh, four separate poultry farms experienced outbreaks of highly pathogenic avian influenza from November 12 to December 18.  Out of 14,984 susceptible birds on the farms, 1,257 birds died from the disease, while the remaining 13,727 bird were culled to prevent the spread of the virus.  The birds tested positive for the H5N1 virus at the Bangladesh Livestock Research Institute and the Field Disease Investigation laboratories.  The source of the outbreaks remains unknown.  Control measures include movement control of poultry and disinfection of affected premises.
