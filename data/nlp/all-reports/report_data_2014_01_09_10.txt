China (H7N9)
The Chinese Centre for Health Protection of the Department of Health confirmed four new outbreaks of the H7N9 virus since the first of the year. The victims, an 86-year-old man from Shanghai, a 34-year-old woman from Zhejiang, a 47-year-old man from Foshan, and a 71-year-old man from Yangjiang have all been hospitalized and are in critical condition. Close contacts with all of the victims are being carefully monitored for flu-like symptoms. To date, a total of 148 human cases of the H7N9 virus have been confirmed on Mainland China. The country has increased surveillance at the borders and the public is being urged to avoid live poultry markets, wash hands frequently, cook poultry thoroughly before eating, and stay away from crowded areas.
