China.
In the Xinjiang region of China an outbreak of the H5N1 flu has killed 1,600 chickens and affected another 5,500 chickens. The outbreak started on June 20 at a farm run by the Xinjiang Production and Construction Corps but was not confirmed until July 2 by the Harbin Veterinary Research Institute. In an effort to contain this outbreak 156,439 chickens have been culled,  and 116327 chickens have been vaccinated. Additional control measures in the Xinjiang region include a quarantine of the infected area and establishment of movement control.
