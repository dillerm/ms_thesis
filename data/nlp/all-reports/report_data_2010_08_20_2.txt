H5N1 in Poultry in Vietnam
 An outbreak of H5N1 bird flu in poultry in Gia Lai province began on July 18. Out of 1900 birds in Ia Hrung village, 760 were ill and 400 died. The remaining birds were culled to prevent the spread of the disease. The source of the outbreak may have been the introduction of new live animals, illegal movement of animals, or contaminated objects. Preventative measures such as quarantine, movement control, disinfection of the premises, and modified stamping out have been put in place.
