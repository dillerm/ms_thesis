Avian Influenza in Wild Animals in Japan
The Environment Ministry reported that a dead duck found Thursday in Koto Ward, Tokyo, has tested positive in a genetic test for an avian influenza virus.  A further test will be conducted by the National Institute of Animal Health to determine whether the virus is highly pathogenic.  If so, it will be the first bird flu case in Tokyo.  The ministry has designated a 10-km radius from where the bird was found as a priority monitoring zone.A whooper swan found Wednesday in Kurihara City, Miyagi Prefucture, has tested positive for avian influenza, the Environment Ministry reported.  The ministry has decided to test more wild birds in order to determine whether they are carrying the highly pathogenic virus, and has asked all citizens to avoid contact with wild birds.
