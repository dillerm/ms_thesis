H5N1 in Wild birds in Japan
According to a report submitted to the OIE on June 29, H5N1 bird flu virus has not been isolated from wild birds since May 9, 2011. By now, winter migratory birds have almost all returned to the north. Regular surveillance for H5N1 in wild birds has been conducted all year around. The event is resolved and no more reports will be submitted. No H5N1 bird flu has been detected in domestic poultry since March 16, 2011. Following culling and disinfection activities, H5N1 has not been detected in subsequent monitoring efforts for three months and the country is declared H5N1-free as of June 25, 2011.
