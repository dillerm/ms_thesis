Egypt (HPAI H5N1)
A five-year-old Egyptian child died from bird flu and is the tenth death from the virus in the country out of 23 identified cases this year. The child's mother said he had contact with sick birds. An infected 30-year old woman from the Menoufiya governorate was admitted to hospital and is still undergoing treatment. Finally, a 25-year-old woman from Minya province who developed symptoms on 12/21 and was hospitalized two days later have all been confirmed to be infected with HPAI H5N1. This brings the total to 24 cases this year with 10 deaths. Out of the 14 other surviving infected patients, eight have been discharged and six others are still sick in hospital. According to the World Health Organization (WHO), from 2003 to Oct. 2, 2014, 668 laboratory-confirmed human cases of H5N1 infection have been officially reported by 16 countries. Of these cases, 393 have died. Egypt's H5N1 cases have largely been in poor rural areas in the south, where villagers, particularly women, tend to keep and slaughter poultry in the home. Bird flu first appeared in Egypt in 2006.
