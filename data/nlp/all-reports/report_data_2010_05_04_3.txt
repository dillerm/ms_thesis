H5N1 in Humans in Indonesia
 A four-year-old girl from Riau province was first hospitalized from April 21-23 but was sent home and then rushed to the hospital again on April 24 because of a high fever and difficulty breathing. She was later transferred to Arfin Achmad hospital and placed in the bird flu isolation unit. The girl died on April 28 and tests returned positive for H5N1 bird flu on May 1.   The girl had not been in direct contact with infected poultry. Three others are in intensive care at Arfin Achmad hospital for suspected bird flu, a 40-year-old woman, and two girls, 14 and 18 respectively.. They were all placed in isolation because of high fevers but are now in stable condition.An 11-year-old boy from West Sumatera province is being treated for suspected bird flu. Dozens of chickens died in his neighborhood, and tests by the Health and Livestock Services have confirmed H5N1 bird flu as the cause. During the outbreak, the boy was brought to Lubukbasung hospital with bird flu-like symptoms. “We observe his progress. If necessary, we will refer him to M Djamil hospital in Padang to get better service,” said the authority of Kecamtan Ampek Nagari, Welvizar. Control measures have been taken to prevent the spread of disease from the chickens such as burning and burying them.
