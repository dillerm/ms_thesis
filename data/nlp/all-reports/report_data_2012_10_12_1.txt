H5N2 in Poultry in South Africa
Earlier this month, officials announced that an outbreak of the highly pathogenic avian influenza H5N2 virus had broken out on a commercial ostrich farm in Hessequa, a municipality located in the Western Cape Province of South Africa. A total of 1,930 birds were potentially affected and the culling of ostriches on the infected farm is taking place. The source of these outbreaks remains unknown. Control measures applied include quarantine and screening to monitor any possible future outbreaks. In the Western Cape provinces of Stellenbosch and Hessequa, the OIE Animal Health Information Department recently reported a case of the low pathogenic avian influenza H5N2 virus. One ostrich died and a total of 1,952 birds were potentially affected. The source of these outbreaks remains unknown. Control measures applied include quarantine, movement control, and screening to monitor any possible future outbreaks.
