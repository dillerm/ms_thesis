H5N1 in Humans in Indonesia
Indonesian Health Minister Siti Fadillah Supari has halted avian influenza surveillance activities at the Naval Medical Research Unit-2 (NAMRU 2) laboratory operated by the U.S. in Jakarta.  "They are not allowed to do any activities anymore," she said. "The term is suspension, but there are no activities at all now." The US NAMRU lab had been an essential resource helping Indonesia to detect H5N1, but became embroiled in Indonesia's refusal to share influenza virus information with other countries. A memorandum of understanding expired two years ago and has not been renewed, but Retno Marsudi, Indonesian directorate general for America and Europe relations, said that the contract with the U.S. to operate the lab has not been terminated.(more...)
