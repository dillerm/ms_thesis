H5N1 in Humans in Egypt
The Ministry of Health announced two new cases of H5N1 bird flu infection in humans on March 12. The first case is a pregnant, 20-year-old woman from Qalyubiyah governorate.  She was hospitalized on March 5, treated with oseltamivir, and died on March 9. The second case is a 1 1/2 year old boy from Kafr el Sheik governorate, who was hospitalized on March 2, treated with oseltamivir, and is in stable condition. Investigations into the cause of infection suggest that both patients had been exposed to sick and dead poultry. The Egyptian Central Public Health Laboratories confirmed H5N1 bird flu as the cause of illness in both cases. The ministry also reported the death of a 53-year-old man from Qalioba governorate who had developed symptoms of bird flu on February 27. Across Egypt to date there have been 106 confirmed cases of H5N1 bird flu in humans and 32 fatalities.
