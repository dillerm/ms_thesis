Taiwan (HPAI H5N2)
The Tainan City Animal Health Inspection and Protection Office report that in Taipei on May 4, around 5,000 chickens were culled after an avian influenza outbreak was confirmed. The Animal Health Research Institute under the Council of Agriculture confirmed that on May 2, the dead chicken had been infected with the avian flu virus H5N2 after conducting tissue sample tests. The office also completed the disinfection of the affected farm, other poultry farms within a 1 kilometer radius, and the surrounding paths and highways. As of the time of press, no abnormal poultry deaths had been reported on any other poultry farms. The cause of the outbreak is unknown.
