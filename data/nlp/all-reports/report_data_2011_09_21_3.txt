H5N1 in Poultry in India
 On August 29, a village in the Indian state of Assam experienced an outbreak of H5N1 bird flu. Out of 34,313 birds, 1,436 died and another 15,409 birds were culled to prevent the spread of the virus. Control measures applied include quarantine, movement control, and screening. Although the source of the outbreak is unknown, surveillance, disinfection, and closure of the poultry markets in the infected zone have been implemented.
