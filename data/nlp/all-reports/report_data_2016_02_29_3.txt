Taiwan (HPAI H5N2)
The World Organisation for Animal Health (OIE) reports 4 new cases of highly pathogenic H5N2 avian influenza virus in the counties of Changhua , Pingtung  and Yunlin and in the municipality of Tainan city.  A total of 18,259 birds died from the disease, confirmed by the Animal Health Research Institute, and the remaining 22,866 were culled to prevent it spreading further.  In addition to culling, the following control measures were implemented: movement control; screening; disinfection; quarantine; stamping out; and zoning.  The source of these outbreaks is unknown.
