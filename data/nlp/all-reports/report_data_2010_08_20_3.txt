H5N1 in Humans in Egypt
The Ministry of Health of Egypt has announced that a 2-year-old girl from Cairo is ill from H5N1 bird flu. She developed symptoms on 2 August, was hospitalized on the same day and received oseltamivir treatment. She currently remains under treatment in the hospital. Investigations into the source of illness suggest that the patient was exposed to sick and dead poultry. The Egyptian Central Public Health Laboratories, a National Influenza Center of the WHO Global Influenza Surveillance Network (GISN), confirmed the case. This is the 111th human case of bird flu to be confirmed by laboratory testing in Egypt.
