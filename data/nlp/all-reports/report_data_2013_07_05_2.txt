H5N1 in Poultry in Nepal
 One outbreak of bird flu began on May 28 in Giriraj birds raised in a household in Naranyi Province and two outbreaks have begun in Lumbini Province, one on June 2 in backyard ducks and one in broiler chickens on June 7. A total of 1,675 susceptible birds were housed in the various locations, 410 birds came down with H5N1 birds and died, and 1,165 birds were culled to prevent the spread of the disease. The cause of the outbreaks is unknown, but the premises have been disinfected and surveillance activities are ongoing throughout the country. All of the outbreaks were resolved as of June 14.
