H5N1 in Humans in Egypt
Two new cases of bird flu in humans were announced by the Ministry of Health on February 17. A 32-year-old man from Menofya governate developed symptoms on February 6 and was hospitalized on February 8. He received treatment with oseltamivir and is in stable condition. A pregnant 29-year-old woman, also from Menofya governate, developed symptoms on February 6 and was hospitalized on February 12. She was treated with oseltamivir, but died on February 13. Investigations suggested that both patients had been in contact with sick and dead poultry. The diagnosis was confirmed by the Egyptian Central Public Health Laboratories. Two patients with previously announced cases of bird flu also died from the disease, a 37-year-old man from Helwan governorate and another 29-year-old woman from Menofya governate. An additional two cases of bird flu were announced on February 19, a 13-year-old boy and a 30-year-old woman from Kafr El-Sheikh governorate. The total number of human cases in Egypt since the first outbreak of the disease is now 102.
