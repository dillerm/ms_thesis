H5N1 in Poultry in Mexico
 The Servicio Nacional de Sanidad, Inocuidad y Calidad Agroalimentaria (Senasica) of the United States of Mexico reported on September 12, that in order to contain an outbreak of the H7N3 avian influenza virus, poultry farmers have killed 22.3 million birds between the months of June and August. The outbreak was first detected in the farms in the Los Altos region of the state of Jalisco. Farms are now allowed to restock as it has been three weeks since the last know outbreak with between 4.5 to 5 million birds being added each month.
