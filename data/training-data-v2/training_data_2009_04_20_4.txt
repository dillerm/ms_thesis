H5N1 in Poultry in Germany
The Friedrich-Loeffler-Institut confirmed an outbreak of low-pathogenicity H7N7 that began April 2 at a poultry farm in Kleve. 454 of the 16,700 turkeys raised on the farm died in the outbreak and the rest have been culled. Further control measures such as restricting poultry movement within the country, screening, disinfection of infected premises, and control of wildlife reservoirs have been implemented.
