H5N1 in Humans in Indonesia
Since the H5N1 bird flu outbreak began in Pinrang, South Sulawesi, eight local people have developed symptoms such as fever and coughing, but the Health Service has not announced them as possible bird flu patients. Medical history mentioned that they developed illness after contact with sick birds. The Health Service is observing the progress of these patients.Three residents of Pinrang and one resident of Soppeng are being treated in the Inspection Center of Wahidin Sudirohusodo, Makassar, South Sulawesi. The three patients from Pinrang, a 17-year-old, a 42-year-old, and a 4-year-old, were admitted on September 29. The 3-year-old from Soppeng was admitted the day before. According to the director of Wahidin Sudirohusodo Hospital, Andi Kalsum Patonangi, the patients are suspected to have contracted bird flu infection because they became ill shortly after many chickens died nearby.
