Ghana (H5N1)
On June 28, 2016, the World Organization for Animal Health (OIE) reported 2 H5N1 outbreaks in Ghana.  Dr Kenneth M. K. Gbeddy, Chief Veterinary Officer, Veterinary Services Directorate, Ministry of Food and Agriculture, Accra, Ghana confirmed 3944 birds have died.  A total of 1,764 birds were destroyed in outbreaks in Ashanti province and in the Greater Accra Metropolitan Area.  In addition to destroying susceptible birds, measures implemented were: movement control inside the country, disinfection/disinfestation, quarantine, surveillance outside containment and/or protection zone, stamping out, and official destruction of animal products.
