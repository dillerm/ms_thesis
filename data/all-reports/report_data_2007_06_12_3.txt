H5N1 in Poultry in Malaysia:
The first H5N1 outbreak in over a year has been  detected in a village (Paya Jaras Hilir) in the state of Selangor near the  capital Kuala Lumpur. Sixty chickens have died over a period of three  days according to the head of disease control for the Department of Veterinary  Services.  Tests have confirmed H5N1 in two of the birds. Singapore has  announced that it has banned poultry and eggs imported from Selangor but  not from other regions of Malaysia.  According to reports, Malaysia  exports 3.6 million poultry and 83.7 million eggs per month to Singapore.
