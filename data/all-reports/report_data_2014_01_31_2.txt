China (LPAI H7N9)
The Ministry of Agriculture in Beijing recently reported two outbreaks of H7N9. The first outbreak occurred in an agriculture products markets in Guangzhou, Guangdong. Out of 185 specimens collected, two tested positive for the virus. As result, 1,200 birds were destroyed. The second outbreak occurred at the Meiling agriculture products market in Jinjiang, Quanzhou, Fujian. Here, 220 specimens were collected and one specimen tested positive for the virus. Across the country, a total of 33,400 samples from 2,402 sites have been tested in 2014 and 8 samples (including those above) have tested positive for H7N9.  All were from live bird markets. Control measures being applied to these outbreaks include stamping out, movement control inside the country, zoning, disinfection of infected premises/establishments, dipping/spraying, and the prohibition of vaccination or treatment of affected animals.
