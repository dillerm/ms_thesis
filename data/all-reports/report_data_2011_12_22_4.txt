H5N1 in Humans in Egypt
 The World Health Organization was notified by the Ministry of Health and Population of Egypt of two human cases of highly pathogenic avian influenza infection.  A mother and child in the Dakahlia Governorate of Egypt developed bird flu symptoms on November 26.  The mother, who was 24 weeks pregnant, was admitted to a hospital on December 3, received oseltamivir, but died on December 3.  The child was hospitalized on December 2, received oseltamivir, and is currently in stable condition.  Prior to infection, the mother and child had exposure to both sick and dead backyard chickens and turkeys.  Of the 155 cases of human infection of H5N1 bird flu confirmed in Egypt, 53 have been fatal.
