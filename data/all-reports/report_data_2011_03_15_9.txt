H5N1 in humans. in Egypt
This week, the Ministry of Health of Egypt has confirmed four new cases of H5N1 bird flu infection in humans. The first case was a 32-year-old female from Sharkia Governorate who developed symptoms on February 10, was hospitalized on February 14, and died on March 3.  The second case is a two-year-old boy from Kafr el Sheikh Governorate who developed symptoms on February 18 and was hospitalized on February 20. He is currently under treatment and is in good general condition.  The third case is a 17-year- old female from Behira Governorate who developed symptoms on February 27 and was hospitalized on March 1. She is currently in stable condition. The fourth case was a 17-year-old female from Dakahlia Governorate who developed symptoms on February 24, was hospitalized on February 26 and died on February 28.  In all four cases, bird flu infection was confirmed by Egyptian Central Public Health Laboratory, a National Influenza Center of the WHO Global Influenza Surveillance Network.  The lab determined that all cases were exposed to poultry suspected to have avian influenza.
