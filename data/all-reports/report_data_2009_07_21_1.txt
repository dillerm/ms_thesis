H1N1 News Update in Argentina
An outbreak of the pandemic (H1N1) 2009 virus in pigs began June 24 on an industrial farm in Buenos Aires. About 820 of 5,586 pigs on the farm have confirmed cases. Suckling pigs and castrated pigs have shown mild clinical symptoms such as coughing. An investigation into the origin of the outbreak is ongoing since the swine may have been infected by humans or by animals from other farms. A quarantine zone with a 3 km radius has been established around the premises. No new cases have been observed since June 24.
