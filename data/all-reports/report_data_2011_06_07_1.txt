H5N1 in Poultry in South Korea
An outbreak of H5N1 bird flu that began on May 16 in Gyeonggi province killed 650 chickens on a farm of 15,800. The birds tested positive by PCR conducted at the National Veterinary Research and Quarantine Service. The remaining chickens were culled to prevent the spread of the disease. The cause of the outbreak is yet to be determined since previous outbreaks had been resolved. Control measures have been applied including control of wildlife reservoirs, movement control, screening, zoning, and disinfection of the infected premises.
