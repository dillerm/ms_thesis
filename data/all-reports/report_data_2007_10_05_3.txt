H5N1 in Humans in Indonesia
A 21-year-old man who died on 9/30 is the country's 86th fatal case of bird flu and the total number of cases in Indonesia stands at 107. He is belived to have contracted the disease from infected poultry. His 19-year-old sister who died with similar symptoms in April has been retroactively determined to have died of avian influenza and not typhoid fever.Tthe head of the neighborhood unit said that the two siblings helped their uncle slaughter and clean chickens for sale at the nearby market. Although officially keeping poultry is banned in Jakarta, many residents have ignored the ban. In a raid in the area following the recent human case, the authorities found over 100 chickens in the neighborhood.
