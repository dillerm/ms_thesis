H5N1 in Wild birds in Hong Kong
 The Chiu Lut Sau Memorial Secondary School in Yuen Long closed on Friday, December 16, after a sick black-headed gull found on the school grounds tested positive for H5N1 bird flu.  The black-headed gull died on December 13 the day after it was found.  The Agriculture, Fisheries & Conservation Department said there were no unusual number of deaths or signs of avian influenza in local poultry farms and wholesale markets.Similarly, a Oriental magpie robin was found dead at the Ju Ching Chu Secondary School in Yuen Long on December 17.  The dead robin tested positive for highly pathogenic avian influenza in a preliminary examination.  Officers from the Agriculture, Fisheries & Conservation Department have inspected two chicken farms in close vicinity of the school but found no bird flu symptoms.  These farms will be put under enhanced surveillance.
