H5N1 in Humans in Indonesia
 In the Yogyakarta province of Indonesia, the Ministry of Health of Indonesia notified the World Health Organization on August 10, 2012, of a new human case of H5N1 bird flu.  The victim, a 37 year old man, developed a fever on July 24, was hospitalized three days later, and died on July 30.  He had four pet caged birds in his home, which was close to both a poultry slaughter house and a farm.  Infection with the A(H5N1) virus was confirmed by the National Institute of Health and Development.  There have been eight human cases of highly pathogenic avian influenza in Indonesia this year, all of them fatal.
