Belgium (HPAI H5N8)
Highly pathogenic H5N8 avian influenza was detected in Maffle, Belgium on June 20, 2017. The outbreak caused the death of 57 birds and the additional culling of 110. The outbreak was caused by the introduction of new live animals. The event is considered ongoing and control measures applied include surveillance outside containment, traceability, stamping out, control of wildlife reservoirs, zoning, vaccination prohibition, and no treatment of affected animals.
