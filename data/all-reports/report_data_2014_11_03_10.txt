Avian Influenza in Humans in China (LPAI H7N9)
China's National Health and Family Planning Commission notified the World Health Organization of two new confirmed cases of humans infected with avian influenza A(H7N9) virus, one of whom died, the WHO reported on October 29. The patient who died, a 44-year-old woman from Urumqi City in Xinjiang Uyghur Autonomous Region, had a history of exposure to live poultry. She developed symptoms on September 4, was admitted to a hospital on September 7, and died on September 9. The second case, a 7-year-old girl from Chaoyang District in Beijing Municipality, developed symptoms on September 16, but her condition was mild and she was not hospitalized.
