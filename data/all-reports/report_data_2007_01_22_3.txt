H5N1 in Wild Birds in Australia:
Graham Burgess from James Cook University in Australia is launching a   3-year surveillance project to monitor birds, especially waterfowl, in north   Queensland for their potential to transmit H5N1 to Australia.  Between one to   ten percent of the waterfowl in Queensland are expected to carry some form of   avian influenza, but the highly pathogenic H5N1 has not been found in Australia   or in nearby Papua New Guinea.  Dr. Burgess has stated that it is not likely   that infected wild birds will be the way that H5N1 is introduced into   Australia.
