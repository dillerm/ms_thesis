H5N1 in Humans in Indonesia
A 17-year-old boy in West Lombok died from H5N1 bird flu on March 9. He was a construction worker and began experiencing symptoms of fever on February 28 but did not seek treatment until March 1. His health worsened and he was admitted to the Mataram hospital where he died. Laboratory tests were positive for bird flu. A ministry investigation found that the boy had been exposed to a dead chicken shortly before getting sick. His death is the sixth human death from bird flu in Indonesia this year. Worldwide this year, there have been 18 cases of human infection with bird flu in six different countries reported to the WHO. Of these cases, 10 were fatalities.
