Italy (HPAI H7N7)
The World Organisation for Animal Health (OIE) has reported a case of highly pathogenic H7N7 avian influenza virus at a commercial free-range layer farm in the Emilia-Romagna Region.  Testing of samples was performed at the Experimental Zooprophylactic Institute.  At total of 600 hens died of the disease, and the remaining 16,400 were culled to prevent it spreading further.  In addition to culling, control measures include: disinfection; traceability; stamping out; surveillance; zoning; and official disposal of carcasses, by-products and waste.  The cause of this outbreak is unknown.
