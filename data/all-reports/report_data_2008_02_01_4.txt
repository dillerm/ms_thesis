H5N1 in Wild birds in Indonesia
A physician writing to ProMED said that during the investigation into the source of one of the H5N1 outbreaks in Riau province, Indonesia, some of the general public became concerned about a nearby swallow farm (possibly cave swiftlets, Collocalia fuciphaga, being raised for their edible nests) as a possible source of the virus. Some people burned tires to scare the birds away from a village building used for their nests.  However, within the village (Burung Angsa), testing revealed that eight out of eight geese did test positive for bird flu, though they did not appear to have any symptoms.  The writer concludes that the blame for bird flu “seemed to follow the momentum of the community. There is a quickness to reach a cause or diagnosis, then stick to that despite data to the contrary.” (more...)
