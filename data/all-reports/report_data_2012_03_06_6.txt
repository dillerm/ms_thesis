H5N1 in Humans in Bangladesh
A 40-year-old man was diagnosed with H5N1 bird flu through the live bird market surveillance program in Dhaka City on February 26. The man works in the market and had a cough. Nasal and throat swabs were collected and tested positive for H5N1 bird flu. The man is otherwise free of symptoms. IEDCR (Institute of Epidemiology, Disease Control & Research) and ICDDR,B (International Centre for Diarrhoeal Disease Research, Bangladesh) are jointly investigating the case and monitoring the situation. This is the 4th case of human H5N1 bird flu in Bangladesh since the disease was first reported there in 2008.
