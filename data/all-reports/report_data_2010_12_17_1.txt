H5N1 in Wild birds in South Korea
Quarantine officials found 2 dead eagle owls that were infected with the H5N1 bird flu in the west central part of the country on December 10. The Farm Ministry reports that since the disease has not been found in any domestic poultry, the country is still considered free from infection and exports will not be affected. The bird carcasses were found near Seosan in the northern part of the country. No human cases have been reported in the country so far.
