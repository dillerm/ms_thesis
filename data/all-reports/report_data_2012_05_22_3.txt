Taiwan
In Bei-gang Township, Yunlin County, an outbreak of the HPAI H5N2 virus has been confirmed by agricultural authorities. From the start of the outbreak on May 7 to May 12, nearly 4,000 chickens died from this outbreak. More than 11,000 chickens were culled as a control measure to control the infection. Additional control measures employed include movement control and quarantining the affected population. Testing from the Animal Health Research Institute of Taiwan has confirmed the diagnosis through gene sequencing. Separately, in the county of Taichung, Taiwan, 176 ducks were culled as part of an ongoing effort to control an LPAI H5N2 outbreak. Additional control measures that are being employed include movement control and quarantine.
