H5N1 in wild birds.
Seven new cases of H5N1 bird flu in Japan were reported to OIE this week; 3 in Tottori, 1 in Hokkaido, 1 in Hoyago, 1 in Nagasaki, and 1 in Miyazaki district.  A total of seven dead wild birds tested positive for bird flu between February 24 and March 3: 1 great crested grebe (Podiceps cristatus), 1 whooper swan (Cygnus cygnus), 1 peregrine falcon (Falco peregrines), 1 pochard (Aythya ferina), 1 tufted duck (Aythya fuligula), and 2 mandarin ducks (Aix galericula).  The source of the outbreak is unknown, but wild birds are being screened for the disease.
