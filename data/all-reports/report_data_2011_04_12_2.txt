H5N1 in Poultry in Indonesia
 The Health Service and Livestock and Food Security Service of Gorontalo in North Sulawesi culled hundreds of H5N1bird flu-infected chickens on April 4 after about 195 chickens in 5 villages in Gorontalo tested positive for H5N1 bird flu virus on April 2.  Residents suspected bird flu infection when they found dead chickens with a bluish appearance.  Following the discovery, they collected and culled all birds within a 100-meter radius of the infected spot. Culling was conducted by slaughtering then burning the chickens.  The local government is now screening all villages within Gorontalo city to prevent the disease from spreading. At the time of the report, there were no human infections in the area.
