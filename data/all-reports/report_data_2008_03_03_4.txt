H5N1 in Poultry in India
The UN praised India for its “swift and comprehensive” measures to bring the outbreak of bird flu in West Bengal under control. Food and Agriculture Organization's veterinary expert Mohinder Oberoi said that intensive culling of the predominantly backyard poultry operations in West Bengal appears to have stopped the outbreak, although the possibility of reccurrence remains high.  FAO’s Chief Veterinary Officer Joseph Domenech said "The virus could still be present in the environment despite heavy slaughtering and extensive disinfection of affected areas, or it could be reintroduced from other countries."
