H5N1 in poultry. in South Korea
 5 new cases of highly pathogenic  H5N1 bird flu in South Korea were reported to OIE this week: an outbreak in Sangbong, Icheon city, Gyeonggi began on Feb 17; the one in Naesong, Yangsan city, Gyeonggi on Feb 24; an outbreak  in Gwangdae, Yeonju district, Gyeonggi on Feb 23; the one in Banryong, Damyang district, South Jeolla on Feb 24; and the outbreak in Yongdong, Goheung district, South Jeolla on Mar 1st.   A total of 205 chickens and 2,100 ducks tested positive for bird flu between March 3 and March 10.  The source of the outbreak is unknown.  In an effort to control the spread of the outbreak, poultry in the affected farms and in the area within 500-m radius from the affected farms were culled and buried, and disinfection and clinical surveillance in the area within a 10-km radius from the affected farms were intensified.A new case of low pathogenic H7N2 bird flu in Samdam, Iksan city, Jeolla, was reported to OIE this week.  A total of 600 chickens tested positive for bird flu and were exterminated to control the spread of the outbreak. The source of the outbreak is unknown.The National Veterinary Research and Quarantine Service (NVRQS) confirmed a new case of H5N1 bird flu at a duck farm in the central part of the country on March 5.  Tests showed that 12,400 birds at a poultry farm in Cheonan, 92 kilometers south of Seoul, were infected with the virus. This is the second case of highly pathogenic avian influenza reported in the country this month. The National Veterinary Research and Quarantine Service (NVRQS) also confirmed a new case of H5N1 bird flu at a chicken farm in Yongin, 50 kilometers south of Seoul, later in March. Tests showed the 200,000 of the birds at the large egg-laying farm were positive for the virus, making it the third case of the virus reported in the country this month.  In both cases, all birds will be culled with quarantine authorities asking nearby farms to be vigilant on protecting their birds.
