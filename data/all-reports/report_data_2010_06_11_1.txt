H5N1 in Wild birds in China
According to a statement released June 9 by the Information Office of the Ministry of Agriculture, the carcasses of wild birds from Nagqu Prefecture in Tibet tested positive for H5N1 bird flu at the National Bird Flu Reference Laboratory. Soon after learning of the results, the Ministry of Agriculture sent experts to help the local Department of Forestry with preventative measures such as monitoring wild birds in the prefecture and informing local farmers and herdsmen of  related measures. A total of 160 wild bird carcasses including 141 brown-headed gulls, 27 bar-headed geese, 1 chough and 1 widgeon had appeared in the area from mid-May to May 25.
