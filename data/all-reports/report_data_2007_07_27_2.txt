H5N1 in Poultry in India
The joint secretary of the Ministry of Health has confirmed that  H5N1 has been found in chickens on the east side of the subcontinent. This is  the first outbreak of bird flu in India in a year. Government workers plan to  slaughter about 150,000 poultry in a 5-km radius around the affected farm in  Manipur state, which lies on the border with Myanmar. Health workers will check  for signs of flu among thousands of people around the poultry farm in  Chingmeirong village on the outskirts of Imphal, the state capital, 1,700 km from New Delhi. Neighboring Bangladesh,  Myanmar and Pakistan have also witnessed outbreaks of the H5N1 strain of bird  flu this year in poultry.
