H5N1 in Poultry in Cambodia
An outbreak of H5N1 in some backyard poultry in a village in Kang Cham province began on December 16. The village has a total of 1,012 chickens and 204 ducks. 143 birds died from the disease and another 875 were culled to prevent the spread of the virus. Other preventative measures have been enacted, such as movement control within the country, disinfection of affected premises, and modified stamping out. Tests at the Pasteur Institute in Cambodia and the National Veterinary Research Institute confirmed H5N1 bird flu.
