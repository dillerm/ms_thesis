H5N1 in Wild Birds in Hong Kong:
A bird, possibly a red-billed starling (Sturnus  sericeus), has tested positive for H5N1.  The dead bird was found on  May 26 in the densely populated section of Mong Kok, Kowloon, China and is  undergoing further tests to determine the neuraminidase subtype and  pathogenicity.  A total of 15 wild birds have tested positive for H5N1 in  China so far in 2007.
